# A Triadic Interaction Model with Activity-Verified Identity for Multi-Domain Gig Economy Platforms

## Abstract
The rapid growth of the gig economy has enabled flexible work opportunities across various sectors. However, existing platforms predominantly adopt a two-sided model, focusing on transactions between service providers and consumers, often neglecting the operational and coordination aspects required to sustain recurring and scalable activities. This limitation is particularly evident in event- and activity-based services such as coaching, training, and community-driven engagements, where structured coordination plays a critical role.

This paper proposes a triadic interaction-based platform model that integrates three key actors: skill providers, service consumers, and activity operators. The model introduces activity-oriented execution as the fundamental unit of interaction, supported by an activity-verified identity system that captures participation history, interaction records, and progressive engagement. This identity-centric approach enables gig workers to evolve from individual participants into organized service entities through continuous and structured activity involvement.

Furthermore, the model supports multi-domain participation, allowing users to engage across diverse activity contexts within a unified identity framework. The proposed framework emphasizes interaction orchestration, identity evolution, and scalable participation. A conceptual architecture and use-case scenarios are presented to demonstrate the applicability of the model. This work contributes to the development of next-generation gig economy systems by introducing a structured, identity-driven, and multi-actor interaction paradigm.

**Keywords:** Gig Economy, Triadic Interaction, Verified Identity, Activity-Based Platform, Multi-Domain Systems, Digital Identity

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Background
The gig economy has transformed the nature of work by enabling individuals to provide services on a flexible and on-demand basis. Digital platforms have played a significant role in facilitating interactions between service providers and consumers. Platforms such as Upwork and Airbnb have demonstrated how digital systems can scale service distribution globally (Kässi & Lehdonvirta, 2018; Sundararajan, 2016).

Despite these advancements, most gig economy platforms operate under a two-sided interaction model that focuses primarily on transactional exchanges. This approach is effective for discrete tasks but insufficient for activity-based services such as coaching, workshops, and community programs, which require structured coordination, scheduling, and recurring engagement.

Additionally, identity representation in existing platforms remains largely self-declared. Users present profiles, skills, and experiences that may not be fully verifiable, creating a gap between perceived identity and actual participation. This limitation affects trust, progression tracking, and long-term ecosystem development.

### 1.2 Problem Statement
Existing gig economy platforms face several limitations:
1. Lack of integration of operational roles in interaction models  
2. Limited support for structured and recurring activities  
3. Identity systems based on self-declared information  
4. Absence of mechanisms to track progression and real participation  

### 1.3 Research Objective
This study aims to:
- Propose a triadic interaction model integrating providers, consumers, and operators  
- Introduce activity as the fundamental unit of interaction  
- Develop an activity-verified identity system  
- Enable scalable and multi-domain participation  

### 1.4 Contribution
The contributions of this paper include:
- A triadic interaction framework for gig economy systems  
- An activity-verified identity model  
- A multi-domain interaction approach  
- A conceptual architecture supporting scalable growth

## 2. Literature Review

### 2.1 Gig Economy Platforms
Gig economy platforms such as Upwork and Airbnb operate on a two-sided interaction model connecting providers and consumers (Kässi & Lehdonvirta, 2018). While effective for transactional services, they lack support for structured, recurring, and activity-driven interactions (Sundararajan, 2016).

### 2.2 Event-Based Platforms
Platforms like Eventbrite support scheduling and participation in events. However, they treat events as isolated instances and lack mechanisms for identity progression and continuous engagement (Getz & Page, 2016).

### 2.3 Digital Identity and Authenticity
Existing identity systems rely on profiles and self-declared data. Social media platforms emphasize perception-driven identity, while professional platforms provide structured but still self-reported information (Boyd & Ellison, 2007). These systems lack verifiable linkage to real-world activity.

### 2.4 Research Gap
The literature reveals key gaps:
- Absence of triadic interaction models  
- Lack of activity-based identity systems  
- No unified framework for multi-domain participation  
- Limited support for scalable progression of gig workers

## 3. Proposed Model

### 3.1 Model Overview
The proposed model introduces:
- Triadic interaction structure  
- Activity-oriented execution  
- Activity-verified identity  


### 3.2 Triadic Interaction Structure
The model consists of:
- Skill Providers  
- Service Consumers  
- Activity Operators  

#### Table 1. Roles and Responsibilities in Triadic Interaction

| Actor | Primary Role | Responsibilities | Output |
|------|--------------|------------------|--------|
| Skill Provider | Delivers value | Create/lead activities, provide expertise | Activity delivery, outcomes |
| Service Consumer | Receives value | Join activities, participate, provide feedback | Participation records |
| Activity Operator | Orchestrates interaction | Schedule, manage capacity, coordinate participants | Structured execution |

### 3.3 Activity as Core Unit
Activities (events/sessions) are defined as the fundamental unit, supporting scheduling, participation, and repeatability.

### 3.4 Activity-Verified Identity
Identity is constructed from:
- Recorded participation  
- System-validated interactions  
- Progressive engagement  


### 3.5 Identity Evolution
Users evolve through roles:
Participant → Contributor → Provider → Operator

#### Figure 3. Identity Evolution Model

```

     ↓

     ↓

```

This figure illustrates the progressive role transition enabled by activity-verified identity, where users evolve through participation, contribution, and increasing responsibility. Each transition is triggered by accumulated, verifiable activity records rather than self-declared status.

## 4. Use Case and Discussion

### 4.1 Fitness Ecosystem
A coach conducts training sessions managed by an operator, with participants joining activities. Participation is recorded, and identity evolves over time.

### 4.2 Multi-Domain Participation

- Join seminars  
- Organize art exhibitions  

All activities contribute to a unified identity.
