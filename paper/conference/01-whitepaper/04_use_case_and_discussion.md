# 4. Use Case and Discussion

## 4.1 Fitness Ecosystem
A coach conducts training sessions managed by an operator, with participants joining activities. Participation is recorded, and identity evolves over time.

## 4.2 Multi-Domain Participation
Users may attend fitness sessions, join seminars, and organize art exhibitions within the same platform environment. Even though these activities belong to different domains, all of them contribute to a unified identity that reflects the user's broader history of participation and responsibility.

## 4.3 Continuous Engagement
The system creates a continuous loop in which activity leads to participation, participation leads to recorded evidence, recorded evidence strengthens identity, and identity in turn opens the way for new activities and opportunities.

## 4.4 Comparison with Existing Platforms
Compared with traditional gig platforms, the proposed model moves beyond a two-sided interaction pattern and adopts a triadic one. It also differs from conventional event platforms by treating activity, rather than the isolated event, as the central unit of work. In identity terms, traditional gig platforms tend to rely on profile-based representation, while event platforms provide only minimal identity continuity. The proposed model instead uses activity-verified identity as a core feature. Progression, which is often external or weakly supported in other platforms, becomes built into the system itself. The model also supports multi-domain participation rather than limiting users to a single context, and it places coordination at the center of the platform rather than treating it as a secondary concern.

## 4.5 Discussion
Overall, the model shifts the platform logic from transaction to interaction, from profile-based representation to verified identity, and from isolated activity to a continuous ecosystem of participation and progression.
