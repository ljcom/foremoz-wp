# A Triadic Interaction Model with Activity-Verified Identity for Multi-Domain Gig Economy Platforms

## Abstract
The rapid growth of the gig economy has enabled flexible work opportunities across various sectors. However, existing platforms predominantly adopt a two-sided model, focusing on transactions between service providers and consumers, often neglecting the operational and coordination aspects required to sustain recurring and scalable activities. This limitation is particularly evident in event- and activity-based services such as coaching, training, and community-driven engagements, where structured coordination plays a critical role.

This paper proposes a triadic interaction-based platform model that integrates three key actors: skill providers, service consumers, and activity operators. The model introduces activity-oriented execution as the fundamental unit of interaction, supported by an activity-verified identity system that captures participation history, interaction records, and progressive engagement. This identity-centric approach enables gig workers to evolve from individual participants into organized service entities through continuous and structured activity involvement.

Furthermore, the model supports multi-domain participation, allowing users to engage across diverse activity contexts within a unified identity framework. The proposed framework emphasizes interaction orchestration, identity evolution, and scalable participation. A conceptual architecture and use-case scenarios are presented to demonstrate the applicability of the model. This work contributes to the development of next-generation gig economy systems by introducing a structured, identity-driven, and multi-actor interaction paradigm.

**Keywords:** Gig Economy, Triadic Interaction, Verified Identity, Activity-Based Platform, Multi-Domain Systems, Digital Identity
