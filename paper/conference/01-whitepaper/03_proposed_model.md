# 3. Proposed Model

## 3.1 Model Overview
The proposed model introduces a triadic interaction structure, an activity-oriented mode of execution, and an activity-verified identity layer. Together, these elements define a platform logic that is not limited to one-time transactions, but instead supports coordination, continuity, and the accumulation of verified participation history.

## 3.2 Triadic Interaction Structure
The model consists of three primary actors: skill providers, service consumers, and activity operators. Skill providers deliver value by creating or leading activities and contributing their expertise to execution. Service consumers receive value by joining activities, participating in them, and generating participation records through their engagement. Activity operators orchestrate interaction by scheduling activities, managing capacity, and coordinating participants so that execution becomes structured and repeatable.

### Table 1. Roles and Responsibilities in Triadic Interaction
In this triadic structure, the skill provider functions as the actor who delivers value, the service consumer functions as the actor who receives value, and the activity operator functions as the actor who orchestrates the overall interaction. Their respective outputs take the form of activity delivery and outcomes, participation records, and structured execution.

## 3.3 Activity as Core Unit
Activities (events/sessions) are defined as the fundamental unit, supporting scheduling, participation, and repeatability.

## 3.4 Activity-Verified Identity
Identity in the proposed model is constructed from recorded participation, system-validated interactions, and progressive engagement over time. Rather than relying on static self-description alone, the model ties identity to observable and verifiable involvement in platform activities.

## 3.5 Identity Evolution
Users evolve through roles in a gradual sequence that moves from participant to contributor, from contributor to provider, and eventually from provider to operator.

### Figure 3. Identity Evolution Model


This figure illustrates the progressive role transition enabled by activity-verified identity, where users evolve through participation, contribution, and increasing responsibility. Each transition is triggered by accumulated, verifiable activity records rather than self-declared status.

## 3.6 System Architecture
The system architecture is composed of an interaction layer, an orchestration layer, and an identity layer. These layers work together to capture actor engagement, organize activity execution, and convert operational history into a persistent identity record.
