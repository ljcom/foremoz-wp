# 2. Literature Review

## 2.1 Gig Economy Platforms
Gig economy platforms such as Upwork and Airbnb operate on a two-sided interaction model connecting providers and consumers (Kässi & Lehdonvirta, 2018). While effective for transactional services, they lack support for structured, recurring, and activity-driven interactions (Sundararajan, 2016).

## 2.2 Event-Based Platforms
Platforms like Eventbrite support scheduling and participation in events. However, they treat events as isolated instances and lack mechanisms for identity progression and continuous engagement (Getz & Page, 2016).

## 2.3 Digital Identity and Authenticity
Existing identity systems rely on profiles and self-declared data. Social media platforms emphasize perception-driven identity, while professional platforms provide structured but still self-reported information (Boyd & Ellison, 2007). These systems lack verifiable linkage to real-world activity.

## 2.4 Research Gap
The literature reveals several important gaps. Existing studies do not sufficiently describe triadic interaction models that explicitly include operational actors alongside providers and consumers. They also provide little support for activity-based identity systems, offer no unified framework for multi-domain participation, and rarely address how gig workers can progress in a scalable and verifiable way over time.
